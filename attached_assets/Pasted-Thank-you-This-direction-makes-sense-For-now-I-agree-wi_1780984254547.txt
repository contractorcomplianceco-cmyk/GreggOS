For now, I agree with treating the Gregg + Landon Cockpit as a focused operational cockpit, not a CRM replacement and not just a throwaway prototype.

The system boundary should be:

- The Cockpit owns the follow-through workflow: call notes, reviewed summaries, tasks, escalations, opportunity signals, client ops status, and audit-risk linkage.
- The CRM remains the system of record for client master data, official client history, and long-term account records.

I also agree that Phase 1 should focus on shared data and login before anything else. Gregg and Landon need to see the same live information before the app can be trusted as an operational coordination tool.

Approved direction for planning:

1. Start with shared backend/data foundation
Use the existing api-server structure if it is already present and appropriate. Move away from browser-only/localStorage data.

2. Add login/user identity
We need known users before we can support audit trail, change-awareness, or accountability.

3. Add audit trail and change-awareness after shared data

- What changed
- What is new since last review
